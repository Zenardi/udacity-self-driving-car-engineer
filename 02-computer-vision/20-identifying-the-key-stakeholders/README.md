## Summary

**Identifying Key Stakeholders for Machine Learning Model Development**
====================================================================

This lesson emphasizes the importance of understanding the impact of a machine learning algorithm on various stakeholders involved in its development and deployment.

### Key Concepts

* **Stakeholders**: Individuals or teams who will be impacted by the algorithm, including:
	+ End-users (drivers and passengers)
	+ Engineering teams (UI engineering team, analytical teams)
* **Algorithm purpose**: Understanding the goal of the model helps to identify key stakeholders
* **Impact on experience**: The algorithm's performance affects user satisfaction and experience

### Practical Notes

When building a machine learning model, consider the following practical steps:

* Identify the end-users and their needs: Who will be impacted by the algorithm? What are their pain points?
* Determine the engineering teams involved: Who will be working with the algorithm to display results or make decisions?
* Consider the algorithm's purpose: What problem is it trying to solve? How does it impact the stakeholders?

Example:
```markdown
**Ride-Sharing App Example**

* End-users: Drivers and passengers
* Engineering teams: UI engineering team, analytical teams
* Algorithm purpose: Predicting optimal path between drivers and clients
```
By following these steps, you can ensure that your machine learning model meets the needs of its stakeholders and is developed with their experience in mind.

To help you frame the problem, you need to identify the key stakeholders. Who is going to be impacted by the algorithm that you're building? Maybe this algorithm will be used by analytical teams in your company to better understand patterns. Maybe it will be a critical part of an app or website. It may be client facing or not.

By identifying the key stakeholders, you will gain clarity on the purpose of the model that you're trying to build. Let's consider the following example. You're building a ride-sharing app. In particular, you creating a machine learning model to find the optimal path between the drivers and the clients occasion. Who's going to care about this algorithm?

Well, first of all, the experience of both drivers and passenger will be affected. If your algorithm does not predict the arrival time correctly, the passenger may get frustrated. Similarly, the drivers choice to accept or refuse a ride is impacted by your prediction. Other engineering teams will also be interested in your algorithm, such as UI engineering team, who need to display the most accurate time range possible in the app. This is why identifying the key stakeholders is an essential steps that drives your model development.
