## Tools, Environment & Dependencies
Now that you've built your intuition around localization and motion models, it's almost time to move onto Markov Localization. First, we'll take a look at tools, environment and dependencies you may want to set up (although workspaces will be provided), as well as looping back to Aaron to see a brief overview of the project you will build at the end of the course.

For each exercise throughout the course, you will be provided Udacity workspaces which will have an environment installed for you. However, you may wish to also be able to perform exercises or complete the project in your own local environment. Be forewarned that the environment may be difficult to create.

In that case, you will want to use the following tools and libraries:

* C++ -[VSCode install](https://docs.microsoft.com/en-us/cpp/build/vscpp-step-0-installation) or [general install](https://www.tutorialspoint.com/cplusplus/cpp_environment_setup.htm) (depending on OS, you may already have it)
* [CMake](https://cmake.org/install/)

* Make
 
  * [Windows](http://gnuwin32.sourceforge.net/packages/make.htm)

  * [Mac](https://stackoverflow.com/questions/10265742/how-to-install-make-and-gcc-on-a-mac)

  * [Linux](https://askubuntu.com/questions/161104/how-do-i-install-make)

* [VSCode](https://code.visualstudio.com/download)

, or a similar IDE
* The [CARLA simulator](https://carla.org/)

* [PCL](https://pointclouds.org/downloads/) (Point Cloud Library)

* [Eigen Library for C++](https://eigen.tuxfamily.org/index.php?title=Main_Page)
