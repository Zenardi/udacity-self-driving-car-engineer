## Summary

**Waymo Driver Validation and Safety Framework**
=====================================================

This README file provides an overview of Waymo's comprehensive approach to validating their self-driving technology. The company has made a significant step by publicly releasing their safety framework, which outlines how they develop, evaluate, and manage their vehicles in the real-world.

### Key Concepts

* **Safety Framework**: A document outlining Waymo's approach to ensuring the safety of their self-driving technology.
* **Comprehensive Validation**: A thorough process that includes testing individual system modules as well as the entire system.
* **Simulation Testing**: A method of testing the driver's ability to handle complex situations by simulating tens of billions of miles of driving scenarios.
* **Safety Scenarios**: Thousands of staged scenarios tested at Castle Air Force Base range, including extremely rare events.

### Practical Notes

Waymo tests their technology in multiple ways:

* Individual system modules are tested separately before being integrated into the entire system.
* The system as a whole is also tested through simulation and real-world testing with safety drivers behind the wheel.
* Safety scenarios are staged at Castle Air Force Base range to test rare events.
* Simulation testing is used to check the driver's ability to handle complex situations safely, covering tens of billions of miles.

## Transcript

We validate our Waymo driver in a comprehensive way, which is laid out in the safety framework document that we have made public in October of 2020. This is the first time a company has publicly released such a framework covering how we develop, evaluate, and manage our vehicles in the real-world. One way we test our technology in the real-world is with safety drivers behind the wheel. But even before we get there, we do a wide range of tests. We test every individual system module, but also the system as a whole.

This includes staging thousands of safety scenarios at our Castle Air Force Base range, some of which tests extremely rare events. This also includes extensive simulation testing comprising over tens of billions of miles, where we check that the driver can handle complex situation safely.
