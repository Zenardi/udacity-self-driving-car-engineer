## Summary

**Wave Open Dataset Challenges**
=====================================

The Wave Open Dataset Challenges are a series of competitions focused on advancing autonomous driving technologies. The challenges cover various tasks, including 2D object detection, 3D object detection, 2D tracking, 3D tracking, domain adaptation, real-time detection, and motion forecasting.

**Key Concepts**
---------------

* **Autonomous Driving Technologies**: A set of technologies that enable vehicles to operate independently without human intervention.
* **Wave Open Dataset Challenges**: A series of competitions focused on advancing autonomous driving technologies through various tasks.
* **2D Object Detection**: The task of identifying objects in a 2D image or video frame.
* **3D Object Detection**: The task of identifying objects in a 3D space, such as a point cloud or LiDAR data.
* **Domain Adaptation**: The process of adapting a model trained on one dataset to perform well on another dataset with different characteristics.
* **Motion Forecasting**: The task of predicting the future motion of objects.

**Practical Notes**
-------------------

The Wave Open Dataset Challenges provide a platform for researchers and developers to compare their results against state-of-the-art approaches. The challenges also facilitate collaboration and knowledge sharing among participants from academia, research labs, and industry. Participants can submit their solutions to the challenges, which are then evaluated and ranked on leaderboards that remain open even after the challenge is closed.

## Transcript

In March of 2020, we launched the wave open dataset challenges. These are competition on tasks that are core to autonomous driving technologies such as 2D object detection, 3D object detection, 2D tracking, 3D tracking, as well as domain adaptation. In 2021, we added real-time versions of the detection tasks, as well as two new motion forecasting tasks, which are very important for understanding and predicting the behavior of others. Even after the challenge is closed, our leaderboards remain open and are constantly updating. We have received hundreds of submissions from all over the world, including academia, research labs, and industry.

Ultimately, we hope that with our leaderboards provide a way for the research community to compare their results against the state-of-the-art approaches and to discover the work of others.
