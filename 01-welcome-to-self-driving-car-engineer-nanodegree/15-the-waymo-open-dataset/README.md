## Summary

**Summary of Waymo Open Dataset**

This dataset is a significant contribution to the machine learning community, providing a vast amount of annotated data for autonomous driving research. The dataset consists of two main components: perception and motion datasets.

### Key Concepts

* **Perception Dataset**: A collection of high-resolution images from cameras, including lidar and camera data, annotated with 3D and 2D boxes for objects like vehicles, pedestrians, bicyclists, and signs.
* **Motion Dataset**: Over 100,000 segments of unique driving scenes, corresponding to 570 hours of data, featuring object trajectories and 3D maps.
* **Object Annotation**: Objects are annotated in both 2D and 3D formats for accurate tracking and analysis.
* **Driving Environments**: The dataset covers a wide variety of road types and driving conditions captured around the clock in different urban environments.

### Practical Notes

The Waymo Open Dataset is designed to encourage research into models that can generalize well to new driving environments. By providing a diverse set of annotated data, researchers can develop more robust and accurate autonomous driving systems. The dataset's unique features make it an ideal resource for those working on perception and motion estimation tasks in machine learning.

## Transcript

Waymo is tackling some of the hardest problems that exist in machine learning and is a unique position to contribute to the research community with one of the largest and most diverse autonomous driving datasets ever released. The way open dataset is always changing. As of March 2021, it consists of a perception dataset and a motion dataset. The perception dataset contains high-resolution, lighter and camera data collected by our vehicles, annotated both as 3D and 2D boxes for objects like vehicles and pedestrians, and also bicyclists and signs. The motion dataset consists of more than 100,000 segments, which corresponds to 570 hours of unique data with specifically mindful scenes that contain interesting interactions and provide object trajectories and 3D maps for each scene.

Both datasets feature a wide variety of road types and driving condition captured around the clock in different urban environments such as San Francisco, Phoenix, Mountain View, Range in Seattle and more. We did this to encourage research into models that can better generalize to new driving environments.
