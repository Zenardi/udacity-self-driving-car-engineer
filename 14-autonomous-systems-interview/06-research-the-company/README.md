## Summary

**Researching a Company Before an Interview**
=====================================================

This guide provides essential information on researching a company before an interview. Understanding a company's values, products, and initiatives can help you prepare for common interview questions and demonstrate your interest in the role.

### Key Concepts
* **Company research**: Researching a company's website, job postings, press releases, news articles, and blog posts to gain insight into their values, products, and initiatives.
* **Understanding company culture**: Recognizing a company's commitment to community and its broader focus on transportation, which may include bike-sharing and scooter-sharing services.
* **Identifying competitors**: Knowing who the company considers as competitors, which can be broader than expected (e.g., companies in the mobility and transportation industry).
* **Demonstrating knowledge**: Showing that you're knowledgeable about the company through your research and care about its initiatives.

### Practical Notes
When researching a company before an interview:

1. Review the company's job posting to understand their focus areas.
2. Visit the company's website to learn about their products, services, and community initiatives.
3. Search for press releases, news articles, and blog posts to stay up-to-date on recent developments.
4. Use this information to answer common interview questions, such as "Why do you want to work for this company?" or "What do you know about our products?"
5. Consider using the research to ask informed questions during the interview, demonstrating your interest in the role and the company.

By following these steps, you can show that you're a knowledgeable and enthusiastic candidate, increasing your chances of success in the interview process.
