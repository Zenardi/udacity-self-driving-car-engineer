**Satellite Localization and Positioning**

This summary covers the key concepts discussed in the lesson on satellite localization and positioning.

### Key Concepts
* **Satellite Constellation**: A group of satellites working together to provide location information. At least three satellites are needed for accurate position calculation.
* **GPS Accuracy Limitations**: In robotics applications, such as self-driving cars, GPS accuracy may not be sufficient due to distance limitations.
* **Particle Filter Algorithm**: A technique used in robotics and computer vision to estimate the state of a system from noisy measurements.

### Practical Notes
* To accurately localize a position using satellites, multiple satellites (at least three) are required.
* In robotics applications, such as self-driving cars, GPS accuracy may not be sufficient for precise positioning. Alternative techniques like particle filters can be used to improve accuracy.
* Real-world applications of satellite localization and positioning include robotics, autonomous vehicles, and computer vision systems.
