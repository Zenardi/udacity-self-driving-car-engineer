## Summary

**Vehicle Dynamics and Acceleration**

This README file summarizes key concepts related to vehicle dynamics and acceleration. It covers the essential qualities that need to be considered when evaluating a vehicle's performance.

### Key Concepts

* **Maximum Velocity**: The maximum speed at which a car can travel, which is limited by the vehicle's design and capabilities.
* **Minimum Velocity**: The minimum speed at which a car can move, which corresponds to going backwards. In many cases, it's illegal to drive backwards on public roads.
* **Acceleration**: The rate of change of velocity, which can be divided into two types:
	+ **Lateral Acceleration**: The acceleration perpendicular to the direction of motion, which affects handling and stability.
	+ **Longitudinal Acceleration**: The acceleration parallel to the direction of motion, which depends on the vehicle's powertrain and engine capabilities.
* **Rollover and Skidding Prevention**: Maximum lateral accelerations need to be checked to prevent rollover or skidding.
* **Braking Force**: Minimum accelerations correspond to the maximum braking force of a vehicle.

### Practical Notes

When evaluating a vehicle's performance, consider the following practical steps:

1. Check the vehicle's maximum speed and acceleration capabilities.
2. Ensure that the vehicle can maintain stability at high speeds by checking lateral acceleration limits.
3. Verify that the vehicle's powertrain can handle longitudinal acceleration demands.
4. Consider the braking force of the vehicle to determine its minimum acceleration capability.
