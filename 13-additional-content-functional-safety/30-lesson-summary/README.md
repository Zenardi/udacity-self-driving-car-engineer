## Summary

**Hazard Analysis and Risk Assessment Summary**

This README file summarizes the key concepts and steps involved in hazard analysis and risk assessment, as covered in the Udacity lesson. The goal of this process is to identify potential hazards and mitigate risks associated with vehicle systems.

### Key Concepts

* **Vehicle Item Identification**: Identify a specific vehicle item under consideration (e.g., lane assistance system).
* **Driving Situations**: Define various driving scenarios that could lead to hazardous situations.
* **Hazardous Situation Identification**: Imagine malfunctions of the vehicle item that could result in hazardous situations.
* **Risk Assessment**: Evaluate risk based on severity, exposure, and controllability.
* **Safety Goals**: Define what the vehicle item needs to do to remain safe.

### Practical Notes

To apply these concepts in practice:

1. Identify a specific vehicle item under consideration.
2. Develop a list of driving situations that could lead to hazardous situations.
3. Imagine potential malfunctions of the vehicle item and assess their risk using the severity, exposure, and controllability criteria.
4. Define safety goals for the vehicle item based on the risk assessment results.

Note: The next lesson will refine the safety goals by determining which subsystems are responsible for meeting them, resulting in a functional safety concept document.

### Lesson Summary
### Five Steps to a Hazard Analysis and Risk Assessment

To summarize, there are five steps to the hazard analysis and risk assessment:
* Conduct a situational analysis
* Identify malfunctions
* Combine situations and malfunctions and assess risk
* Determine ASILs
* Derive safety goals

In the lesson, we have done a hazard and risk analysis for both the lane departure warning and the lane keeping assistant. In the project, your job will be to document what we have discussed in this lesson. And then you will need to do two more analyses to show us what you have learned.
