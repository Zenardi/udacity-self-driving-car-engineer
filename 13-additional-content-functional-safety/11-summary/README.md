## Summary

**Summary of Functional Safety in Automotive Systems**

This summary covers the key concepts and principles of functional safety in automotive systems, including hazard identification, risk evaluation, and mitigation. The goal is to reduce risks to reasonable levels and ensure that electronic systems meet acceptable standards.

### Key Concepts

* **Safety**: Reducing risks to reasonable levels by identifying hazards and evaluating their potential impact.
* **Hazard Identification**: Identifying potential sources of accidents or harm in electronic systems.
* **Risk Evaluation**: Assessing the likelihood and potential consequences of each hazard identified.
* **Functional Safety**: Reducing risk in electronic systems, particularly in the automotive industry.
* **ISO 26262**: A guideline for achieving automotive functional safety, based on a standard philosophy model.

### Practical Notes

The lesson emphasizes the importance of identifying hazards and evaluating risks to ensure safe systems. While no specific code or formulas are provided, the concept of hazard identification and risk evaluation is crucial in developing safe electronic systems. In practice, this involves:

* Identifying potential sources of accidents or harm in electronic systems
* Assessing the likelihood and potential consequences of each hazard identified
* Implementing measures to reduce risks to acceptable levels

## Additional Content

### Summary of Functional Safety

The ISO 26262 functional safety standard follows the V model. In functional safety these are the four main steps involved in functional safety according to the standard:
- **Requirements engineering** Define what the system is going to do
-  **Designing or modifying a system architecture** Design what the system will look like
- **Test the system** to make sure it behaves as expected
- **Integrate the system** into larger systems

Please note that requirements engineering activities are performed at all levels on left side of the V and are described in ISO 26262-8

### What the Functional Safety Module is About


This module will mostly focus on the left side of the V model, which includes planning and designing functionally safe systems. You will take on the role of a functional safety manager.

Remember that this is only half of the V model. The right side of the V model involves implementing the plans and designs through software and hardware engineering. Implementations also need to be tested to make sure that they follow the design specifications from the left side of the V.
