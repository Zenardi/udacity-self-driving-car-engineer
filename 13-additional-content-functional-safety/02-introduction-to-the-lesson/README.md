## Summary

**Functional Safety in Automotive Industry**
=============================================

This project introduces the concept of functional safety in the automotive industry, specifically focusing on reducing risk in electronic systems. As a safety manager or engineer, it's essential to ensure that product functions do not lead to injury or harm.

### Key Concepts

* **Functional Safety**: Ensuring that product functions do not lead to injury or harm.
* **ISO 26262**: A standard used in systems engineering to methodically reduce risk in passenger vehicle E/E-Systems.
* **Automotive Safety**: A broader concept encompassing functional safety, which is just one part of the overall automotive safety framework.
* **Functional Safety Analysis**: A systematic approach to identifying and mitigating risks in electronic systems using the ISO 26262 standard.

### Practical Notes

This project provides a foundation for understanding functional safety in the automotive industry. The following lessons will delve into the details of applying the ISO 26262 standard to reduce risks in passenger vehicles. If you're new to functional safety, this is an excellent starting point for learning about the basics and building blocks of carrying out a functional safety analysis according to the ISO standard.

Note: This project does not include any code or practical steps specific to implementing functional safety. However, it provides a solid introduction to the key concepts and standards that underpin the field.

## Lesson Introduction
**Note that functional safety and ISO 26262 do not consider all risk in E/E systems. They only consider reducing risk as a result of malfunctioning behavior of E/E systems.**
#### Functional Safety Standards

Functional safety grew out of the increasing use of E/E (Electronic and Electrical) systems for control systems. Currently, functional safety is used across many industries including avionics, automotive, railroad and the medical device industries.

The most generic standard is [IEC 61508](http://www.iec.ch/functionalsafety/), which originated from industrial markets.  It currently exists as a standard in the IEC/ISO basic safety publication, which covers "general functional safety," for a number of industries.  ISO 26262 specifically applies to automotive passenger vehicle electrical and electronic systems. The [ISO 26262 standard](http://webstore.ansi.org/RecordDetail.aspx?sku=ISO+26262+-+Road+Vehicles+Functional+Safety+Package) is an offshoot of the IEC 61508 standard.

Note that what is described in this video and the lessons that follow encompasses not only FUSA but also Safety of the Intended Functionality [(SOTIF)](https://www.iso.org/standard/70939.html).
