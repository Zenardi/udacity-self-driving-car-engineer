## Summary

**Hazard Analysis and Risk Assessment for Functional Safety**
===========================================================

This lesson introduces the concept of hazard analysis and risk assessment, a critical process in ensuring functional safety as formalized by ISO 26262. The ultimate goal is to reduce risks to levels acceptable by society.

### Key Concepts

* **Hazard Analysis**: Identifying what could go wrong with an item or system.
* **Risk Assessment**: Measuring the risk associated with unintended behavior, also known as a malfunction.
* **Malfunction**: An unintended behavior that can lead to hazardous situations.
* **ASIL (Automotive Safety Integrity Level)**: A classification of the situation and hazard according to severity and probability of occurrence.
* **Driving Conditions**: Situational analysis involves identifying driving conditions, such as city driving in rain.

### Practical Notes

To apply hazard analysis and risk assessment:

1. Identify potential malfunctions that can lead to hazardous situations.
2. Analyze what goes wrong with the item under consideration during situational analysis.
3. Classify the situation and hazard according to severity and probability of occurrence (ASIL).
4. Calculate the ASIL to determine the required safety measures.
5. Define driving safety goals to identify what the system will do to lower risk.

Example: A lane-keeping system malfunctioning by twisting the steering wheel too hard can lead to a hazardous situation, such as vehicle accident. The hazard analysis and risk assessment process helps identify potential malfunctions and their associated risks.
