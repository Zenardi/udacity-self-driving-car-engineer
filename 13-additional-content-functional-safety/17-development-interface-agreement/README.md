## Summary

**Automotive Supply Chain Overview**
=====================================

The automotive industry involves a complex network of companies working together to design, develop, and manufacture vehicles. This summary explains the key players in the supply chain and how they interact with each other.

**Key Concepts**
---------------

* **OEM (Original Equipment Manufacturer)**: A brand name automotive company that sells cars to consumers.
* **Tier 1 Suppliers**: Companies that develop and produce vehicle systems for OEMs, often outsourced by the OEM.
* **Tier 2 Suppliers**: Companies that provide components or services to Tier 1 suppliers.
* **Development Interface Agreement (DIA)**: A document that outlines design and production responsibilities between companies in the supply chain.

**Practical Notes**
-------------------

When working with a complex automotive supply chain, it's essential to have clear agreements in place to avoid disputes and clarify liability. The Development Interface Agreement is a critical component of the safety plan, as it helps identify which company is responsible for fixing safety issues that arise after vehicle sale. This agreement can be used as a template for future collaborations between companies in the supply chain.

Example use case:

* A Tier 1 supplier sources an electronic control unit from a Tier 2 company and uses it to develop an automatic braking system for an OEM.
* The DIA outlines the design and production responsibilities of each company, ensuring that everyone knows their role in the development process.

## Additional Content

### What Goes into a DIA (Development Interface Agreement)?

A DIA (development interface agreement) defines the roles and responsibilities between companies involved in developing a product. All involved parties need to agree on the contents of the DIA before the project begins.

The DIA also specifies what evidence and work products each party will provide to prove that work was done according to the agreement.

The ultimate goal is to ensure that all parties are developing safe vehicles in compliance with ISO 26262.

Here are major sections of a DIA:
* Appointment of customer and supplier safety managers
* Joint tailoring of the safety lifecycle
* Activities and processes to be performed by the customer; activities and processes to be performed by the supplier
* Information and work products to be exchanged
* Parties or persons responsible for each activity in design and production
* Any supporting processes or tools to ensure compatibility between customer and supplier technologies
