## Summary

**Slip Ratio: Understanding Vehicle Dynamics**

The slip ratio is a critical concept in vehicle dynamics that arises from the mismatch between a vehicle's wheel speed and its expected longitudinal velocity. This phenomenon leads to both rolling and slipping motions, which are essential for generating longitudinal force.

### Key Concepts

* **Slip Ratio**: The ratio of the difference between the wheel speed and the expected longitudinal velocity to the wheel speed.
* **Longitudinal Velocity**: The velocity of a vehicle in the direction of motion (e.g., forward or backward).
* **Contact Patch**: The area on the road where the tire meets the surface, responsible for generating forces.
* **Slipping Motion**: A secondary motion that occurs when there's a mismatch between wheel speed and longitudinal velocity.
